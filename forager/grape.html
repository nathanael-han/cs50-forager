Fox Grapes


Vitis labrusca

The wild fox grape plant is a perennial climbing vine. This plant has large wide leaves, and the fruit looks very similar to concord grapes. The grapes are dark purple berries, slightly smaller and less sweet than cultivated grapes. Fox grapes are delicious eaten as a raw snack, or can be made into a flavorful jam. This wild edible has a toxic look alike, so it is important to be very careful to identify this plant correctly. The berry should have multiple round seeds, and the vines have forked tendrils. Its toxic look-a-like, moonseed, has only one crescent-shaped seed, and its vines do not have tendrils.
